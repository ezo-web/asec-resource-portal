## Frequency
The monthly audit should be conducted once per month, ideally within the first week of the month to ensure that financial records are up-to-date and accurate.

## Content
The monthly audit should include the following information:
- A summary of the council's financial transactions for the month (including the same financial details as the transparency report, but for the monthly reporting period)
- A list of any discrepancies or issues found during the audit, along with an explanation of how they were resolved or will be resolved
- Any recommendations for improving the council's financial processes or recordkeeping based on the findings of the audit
- A list of all documents created during the reporting period, including the date of creation, the author, and a brief description of the document's content and purpose
- A list of all documents that were updated or deleted during the reporting period, including the date of update/deletion, the author, and a brief description of the changes made to the document
- A summary of the council's financial position at the end of the reporting period, including any significant changes from the previous reporting period
- A list of internal policies that were created, revised, or retired during the reporting period, along with a brief description of the policy and its purpose
- A list of event proposals that were submitted by consortium delegates, including the date of submission, the proposing club, and a brief description of the proposed event
- A list of all events that were held during the reporting period, including the date of the event, the hosting club, and a brief description of the event's activities and outcomes
- A list of hyperlinks to all meeting notes/summaries that were created during the reporting period, along with the date of the respective meeting
- Web traffic analytics for the public ASEC website and the internal ASEC resource portal (this site)

## Preparation
The monthly audit should be prepared by the Secretary. The data in the audit should be collected using automation software where possible, such as linked Google Forms, Google Sheets, Sheetgo, Google Analytics, extensions, and App Scripts, then prepared in Google Data Studio or Google Docs.

The document should be saved with the following name format: "ASEC Monthly Audit - [Reporting Period]". It should use the document security level INT1 and the use the appropriate watermark.


## Distribution
Once the monthly audit has been prepared, it should be distributed digitally to all ASEC members and the teacher supervisor of ASEC. A physical copy of the audit should be printed for use during internal ASEC meetings, kept with the Secretary, and another physical copy should be given to the school Principal. The monthly audit should not be made accessable to the public, and should only be shared within the council and with the school administration.
