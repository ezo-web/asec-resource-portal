## Frequency
Transparency are to be published twice per semester, on specific dates:
- Fall Report, November 15th
- Winter Report, January 15th
- Spring Report, March 15th
- Summer Report, May 15th

## Content
Transparency reports should include the following information:
- A summary of the council's activities and decisions during the reporting period
- A summary of the council's financials during the reporting period, including:
    - Net income
    - Total expenses
    - Total revenue
    - A list of transactions that occurred during the reporting period, including the date, amount, and description of each transaction
    - Current balance of funds
    - EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization)
    - A breakdown of expenses by category (e.g. events, marketing, etc.)
    - A breakdown of revenue by category (e.g. fundraising, sponsorships, etc.)
    - A comparison of the current reporting period's financials to the previous reporting period's financials, as well as to the same reporting period in the previous year
    - A comparision of the current reporting period's financials to the council's budget for the reporting period, 
    - The council's budget for the next reporting period
    - An explanation of any significant variances between the current reporting period's financials and the previous reporting period's financials, as well as between the current reporting period's financials and the council's budget for the reporting period
- A summary of the council's upcoming plans and initiatives for the next reporting period
- Any other relevant information that the council deems necessary to include

## Preparation
The Vice President is responsible for the transparency report, with support from the Secretary. The report should be prepared with one of the below softwares (based on intended use and preference):
- Google Docs (General)
- Google Data Studio (Data Preparation)
- Canva (Visual Design)

The transparency report should include a cover page with the ASEC logo, the title of the report, and the reporting period. The report should be well-organized and visually appealing, with clear headings and subheadings, as well as charts and graphs to illustrate financial data where appropriate. The report should be written in a clear and concise manner, avoiding jargon and technical language where possible. The report should be reviewed and approved by the Co-Presidents before being published. The report should use the ASEC brand colours and fonts, which should be sourced from the council's template library. When the report has been completed, it should only be exported as a PDF file.

The document should be saved with the following name format: "ASEC Transparency Report - [Reporting Period]". It should use the document security level REL and the use the appropriate watermark.

## Publication
Once the transparency report has been prepared and approved, it should be distributed first to the school administration and the teacher supervisor of ASEC. Afterwards, at least 15 copies of the report should be printed and given to the school library for public access. Finally, a digital copy of the report should be published on the public ASEC website and shared on the council's social media platforms.
